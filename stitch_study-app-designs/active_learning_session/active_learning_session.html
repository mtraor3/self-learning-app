Which economic principle states that as you consume more of a good, the additional satisfaction you get from each new unit decreases?

check_circle

Correct!

Diminishing Marginal Utility is the principle that the utility gained from consuming an additional unit of a good or service is less than the utility gained from the previous unit.

warning

Not quite.

Comparative Advantage refers to the ability to produce goods at a lower opportunity cost, not consumption satisfaction. The correct answer is Diminishing Marginal Utility.
